Service clarity

Garden work grouped by what your yard needs next.

The service menu avoids generic bundles. Each lane is built around a homeowner problem: overgrown beds, thin planting, tired soil, and outdoor spaces that no longer feel intentional.

A

Seasonal bed reset

Weeding, edge recovery, cutback, bed cleanup, and a tidy baseline before planting or ongoing maintenance.

B

Planting plan refresh

Simple plant groupings, spacing notes, bloom rhythm, and replacement ideas for beds that need direction.

C

Soil and mulch preparation

Soil amendment, mulch depth correction, drainage observations, and practical notes for healthier beds.

D

Small outdoor refreshes

Path edges, container groupings, entry beds, porch-side planting, and garden touchpoints that change the feel fast.

Season board

The calendar decides the work, not a canned package.

Garden care changes with temperature, growth pace, and plant stress. The plan stays flexible without becoming vague.

Early season

Clean edges, assess soil, remove winter debris, and map the first planting moves.

Peak growth

Adjust watering notes, manage weeds, shape plantings, and protect bed structure.

Late season

Cutback decisions, mulch checks, division notes, and preparation for the next cycle.

Local fit

A service-area conversation before a calendar promise.

The first exchange confirms location, access, garden size, and the kind of care you need. No broad availability claims are made before the scope is known.

Material feel

Planting direction that feels composed, not crowded.

The visual system is built around texture, spacing, and restraint: fewer scattered choices, clearer groupings, and stronger edges.

Layered greens

Evergreen structure, soft seasonal color, and repeated planting shapes for a quieter bed.

Warm hard edges

Path, pot, mulch, and border choices that make plantings feel intentional without becoming formal.

Open breathing room

Negative space, better spacing, and access lines so maintenance does not fight the design.

Working method

A clear path from messy bed to maintained garden.

The process is intentionally plain-spoken: look closely, decide what matters, do the work in the right order, and leave the owner with care notes.

Walk the garden

Read sunlight, access, water behavior, bed condition, and current plant health.

Set the priority

Separate urgent cleanup from planting ideas, soil work, and future refresh options.

Work in sequence

Clean, prepare, plant, mulch, and shape so each step supports the next one.

Leave care notes

Share maintenance observations and simple next steps for the coming season.

Maintenance intelligence

Care notes that make the garden easier after the crew leaves.

What to water first

New plantings, stressed beds, and container groupings get the clearest notes.

What to leave alone

Some plants need time, not more handling. The notes call that out.

What to revisit

Drainage concerns, thinning areas, and future planting windows are kept visible.
