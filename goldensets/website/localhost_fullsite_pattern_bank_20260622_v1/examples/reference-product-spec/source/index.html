No. 02 The idea

Most headphones have a sound. This one is built not to.

Every decision inside Umbra — the alloy, the damping, the geometry of the chamber — exists to remove a fingerprint, not to leave one. There is no house sound to fall in love with. There is only what was recorded.

What remains is uncomfortably honest: the room a record was cut in, the breath before the first note, the decay you were never meant to notice. Umbra does not flatter the music. It refuses to lie about it.

No. 03 The object

Nothing here is decorative.

Every part you can see is doing acoustic work. Turn it in the light and read it — four decisions you can feel before you ever press play.

01 — Suspension

The weight you stop noticing.

A floating band spreads the load across the crown of your head instead of clamping at two points. After a minute, the headphone is gone — only the second hour tells you it was ever there.

02 — Chamber

Machined, not moulded.

Each cup is cut from a single billet of aluminium, then damped from the inside so it rings at nothing. A plastic shell resonates and colours the sound. Metal, shaped right, simply gets out of the way.

03 — Transducer

The part that does everything, seen by no one.

A single full-range diaphragm, tensioned to move as one piece, with no crossover to smear the timing. One surface, one voice — pistonic where lesser drivers break apart and blur.

04 — Contact

Where it meets you.

The pads seal without pressing, breathe without leaking, and pull off by hand for replacement. The acoustic seal is part of the instrument — so we made the part you touch the part you can renew.

No. 04 The sound

The quietest thing we build is the silence.

Detail doesn't live in the loud parts. It lives in the floor beneath them — the reverb tail, the rosin on the bow, the air moving in the room. Lower that floor and the whole recording stands up.

Umbra is voiced toward that floor: a black, even background that lets the smallest real thing become the loudest new thing you hear.
