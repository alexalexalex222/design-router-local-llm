01 Programs

Four tracks. One progression.

Pick where you want to start. Most members blend tracks once the fundamentals click.

Striking

Boxing and Muay Thai footwork, guard, and combinations drilled on pads and bags, building toward controlled partner work and light technical sparring.

  • Stance, distance, and defensive structure first
  • Pad rounds, bag work, and clinch entries
  • Optional controlled sparring once fundamentals are solid
Format
Group rounds, 60 min
Contact
Scaled to your level
Gear
Wraps & gloves (loaners available)

Grappling

Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu and wrestling fundamentals — positional control, escapes, and submission chains taught through live positional rounds at a safe pace.

  • Positional hierarchy, escapes, and base
  • Gi and no-gi options as you progress
  • Live rounds scaled to experience and consent
Format
Group rounds, 75 min
Contact
Controlled, position-led
Gear
Rashguard or gi (ask at desk)

Youth (14–17)

A focused track for teens covering striking and grappling fundamentals with an emphasis on discipline, conditioning, and respectful partner work.

  • Age-appropriate intensity and supervision
  • Discipline, focus, and conditioning built in
  • Guardian consent required to enroll
Format
Group rounds, 50 min
Contact
Supervised, technical
Gear
Guidance provided on enrollment

Fundamentals

The on-ramp for people who have never trained. Low-pressure sessions that teach movement, terminology, and gym etiquette before you join the main floor.

  • No experience or fitness baseline assumed
  • Learn the warm-up, language, and safety rules
  • Clear path into Striking or Grappling next
Format
Small group, 45 min
Contact
None to light
Gear
Just water & athletic wear

03 First Visit

Your first week, mapped

Nobody gets thrown into sparring on day one. Here is how the first week actually goes.

  1. 1

    Before you arrive

    Book & brief

    Reserve a trial session below. We confirm your slot and tell you exactly what to bring — usually just water and athletic clothes.

  2. 2

    Day one

    Floor tour & warm-up

    A coach walks you through the mat zones, etiquette, and the warm-up so nothing feels unfamiliar before training starts.

  3. 3

    First sessions

    Fundamentals, no pressure

    You drill core movements at your own pace. Partner work is light and supervised — consent and control come first, always.

  4. 4

    End of week one

    Pick your track

    A coach helps you choose Striking, Grappling, or both, and sets a simple weekly plan that fits your schedule and goals.
